The following maps display a contrast between the rates of malarial disease in the Federal Army of the Potomac and its Confederate counterpart from July 1, 1861 to March 31, 1862. This data is taken from Table XXXVI of Charles Smart's Medical and Surgical History of the War of the Rebellion. (Smart, 103) The data on the heat maps displays the total number of malarial cases recorded monthly for each army between these dates. From the map, it can be concluded that the Federal Army of the Potomac suffered more from malarial disease in the late summer months than the Confederate Army of the Potomac.
